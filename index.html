The Mechanical Tele-Vision

(or How the RRG Conquered the World)

-BBC 1935 announced the first regular TV service -two months later, the superior German people launched the Reich Broadcasting Corporation (RRG) before them

Paul Nipkow

German Inventor of Television

-TV was a great German invention -based on Paul Nipkow's creation of a spinning disk that allowed scanning of images -almost 50 years before the credited Amerikaner Farnsworth
-a German woman enjoying early model Mechanical TV with hand cranked Nipkow Disk
-shows the recording, transmission and reception of images and sound
-the inner workings of the reciever -note the many holes on the spinning disk for the light from the neon lamp to pass through -neon glow tube recreated gray tones in the image -lens and hood for viewing the resulting picture
-a German Fernsehsender in action -use of the "flying spot" technique that was in use until the late 1930s (dark room with directed light, photo cells and amplifiers)

Fernseheinheit-sempfänger Telefunken E1

-the originally mass produced German TV cost almost 700 reichmarks -compare this to the KDF-wagen automobile which cost 900 RM -tv was a luxury on the rich could afford

Telefunken receiver from 1933

-since few owned TV, there was initially little interest in the media as a tool beyond the technical achievement of being first -but of course, all this was to change in 1936

At the 1936 Berlin Olympics

-when the Olympic Games game to Germany -first sporting event to be televised -viewed by over 150 000 Germans -this increased interest in broadcasts

Television receiver showing a soccer broadcast in a military hospital in 1942.

-further televised sporting events, party conventions, state visits, interviews with party officials and actors, news reports, cooking shows and lottery drawings followed
"The entertainment programs are particularly curious."
-a turning point came when Gobbels, who was a proponent of radio, was replaced as propaganda chief with someone who saw the true power of the medium -this led to an increase in TV manufacturing and programs on news, sport and education -singers extolling the virtues of SA and SS
"...early reality TV show depicting the wholesome Aryan life of a young German couple for the rest of the population to model themselves on..."
-screens were set up in public places, including in laundries so housewives could tune in -programming to provide a model for the population -show footage of executions of traitors to the Nazis
"We'll be able to show whatever we want. We'll create a reality, which the people of Germany need and can copy." - Josef Hebbels
-cameras followed the German march across Europe -lead to more TVs built and more programming in a variety of languages
-TV not only allowed the Third Reich to capture the hearts and minds of the people, consolidating the gains from their military might
